How to Use
  • Select an EV Mode and ESS Mode to configure how each storage device behaves. Modes can be mixed freely.
  • Click any load node on the diagram (HVAC, Stove, Dryer, Water Heater) to toggle it on or off and see how it affects power flows.
  • Use the Power Settings sliders to set EV charge/discharge rate, solar peak output, and ESS capacity.
  • Set Starting SOC for EV and ESS, then hit ↺ Reset Day to restart the 24-hour simulation from 6 AM with those values.
  • In V2G VPP Variable or Fixed, enter a start and end time for your grid event window. The EV discharges during that window and charges outside of it.
  • Use Peak Rates toggle to force peak or off-peak pricing at any time — useful for testing ESS and V2G behavior without waiting for 4 PM in the sim.
  • The SOC sparklines on the timeline trace EV (green) and ESS (purple) state of charge across the day.
EV Mode Definitions
  • EV Charging Standard one-way charging. EV draws from grid and/or solar. No metering or interconnection requirements beyond a standard EV charger permit.
  • V2G – Savings EV discharges to cover home loads during peak rate windows, reducing grid import. Power stays behind the meter — no export to grid. Requires a bidirectional charger (e.g. GM Energy V2H/Wallbox) and a parallel operating agreement with your utility. Utility meter data (via AMI, meter collar, or V2H kit) must reach the charger so it knows net consumption.
  • V2G VPP – Variable EV responds to a utility dispatch signal, dynamically adjusting output to target zero net grid draw. Requires bidirectional charger, parallel operating agreement, active V2G/V2X program enrollment with GM Energy or aggregator, and real-time meter data via AMI integration, a separate revenue-grade meter kit, meter collar, or the GM Energy V2H kit's built-in CT sensors.
  • V2G VPP – Fixed EV discharges at a pre-committed fixed rate. Home loads are served first; surplus exports to the grid. Same requirements as Variable, plus an export metering agreement and net-metering or export tariff with your utility.
ESS Mode Definitions
  • Backup Only Battery charges to 100% and holds — never discharges during normal operation. Reserved for outage backup. No special interconnection beyond standard ESS permit. Simplest utility approval path.
  • Self Consumption Battery discharges whenever home load exceeds solar production, minimizing grid draw. Charges from solar surplus or off-peak grid. Requires ESS interconnection permit and parallel operating agreement. Meter data is needed to measure net load — typically provided by CT sensors in the ESS gateway (e.g. GM Energy ESS) or a utility-provided smart meter.
  • TOU Discharge Battery charges at full rate during off-peak hours and discharges only during peak rate windows. Behavior is identical to V2G – Savings but from the stationary battery. Requires same metering infrastructure as Self Consumption plus a TOU rate structure from your utility (e.g. PSE EV TOU rate).
Metering & Interconnection Notes
  • AMI integration — utility pushes real-time interval meter data to the charger or home energy system via API. Most common with PSE, Xcel, and other advanced-metering utilities. Lowest hardware cost but depends on utility program availability.
  • Meter collar / meter socket adapter — installs between the utility meter and socket, adding CT sensors to measure whole-home consumption without a separate meter. Used by some V2H kits (e.g. Span, Lumin) when AMI data isn't available.
  • Separate revenue-grade meter — a second utility-approved meter installed at the inverter or charger output. Required by some utilities for export metering in VPP or net-metering programs. Adds cost and requires utility coordination.
  • V2H kit built-in CTs — the GM Energy V2H system includes current transformers that mount inside the main panel, measuring net consumption locally. This is the most practical path for V2G Savings and Self Consumption without AMI or a meter collar.
  • Parallel operating agreement — a utility contract required before any device can operate in parallel with the grid (i.e. export or offset load bidirectionally). Required for all V2G and ESS discharge modes. Often bundled with the interconnection application.
  • Power values in this simulation are illustrative. Actual behavior depends on inverter capability, utility interconnection limits, and GM Energy software configuration.
